History and Evolution of Tractors

The journey from steam engines to modern high-tech tractors shows farming evolution. See what is the history and milestone of India’s top manufacturers.

History Of Tractor

The history of tractors shows how farming has moved from steam engines in the 19th century to today’s smart, electric, and autonomous machines. Early steam and gasoline tractors were heavy and costly, but the rise of diesel engines, rubber tires, and the Ferguson 3-point hitch in the mid-20th century transformed farming efficiency.

Post-World War II, tractor production surged, and hydraulics made them more versatile. In modern times, GPS, automation, and precision farming tools have made tractors smarter and more productive. Looking ahead, electric, hybrid, and driverless tractors promise sustainable and cost-effective farming solutions.

Major Milestones of Tractor Companies in India

  • Mahindra & Mahindra (M&M): Became India’s top-selling brand by 1983 and strengthened its market with Gujarat Tractor Company acquisition in 1999. Reached 3 million tractors in 2019 and 4 million global sales in 2024, celebrating 25 years of the ARJUN series and 60 years of legacy.
  • TAFE: Founded in 1960 with Massey Ferguson, it became India’s largest tractor exporter by 2010. By 2020, it ranked as the world’s third-largest tractor manufacturer and set a Limca record for servicing 614 tractors in a day. Massey Ferguson 244 DYNATRACK won Tractor of the Year in 2021.
  • Escorts Kubota: Began in 1961 with URSUS, partnered with Ford in 1969, and developed indigenous tech from 1977. Unveiled India’s first hybrid tractor in 2019. Renamed Escorts Kubota in 2022, with its FARMTRAC PROMAXX 47 winning Indian Tractor of the Year in 2025.
  • Swaraj Tractors: Founded in 1974, reached 5 lakh production in 2002 and 25 lakh in 2025, offering rugged and affordable tractors for Indian farmers.
  • Sonalika Tractors: Founded in 1995, opened the world’s largest facility in 2017. Recognized among Fortune 500 India in 2024, sold 1,53,764 tractors in 2025, and remained India’s top tractor exporter for six consecutive years.

Key Features of Tractors

Tractors are essential for farming, saving time and effort while boosting productivity. With powerful engines and versatile attachments, they handle tasks from plowing and sowing to hauling and irrigation. Let’s explore the key features that make tractors so effective on the farm.

Engine & Drivetrain

Tractors use strong diesel engines that provide the power needed for heavy loads and tough farm work. This power is effectively transferred to the wheels through a reliable transmission, allowing farmers to control speed and torque for different tasks. Four-wheel drive (4WD) adds extra traction, making it easier to work on wet, uneven, or muddy fields.

Implements & Hydraulics

The hydraulic system works with the engine to lift and operate various tools and implements, making fieldwork easier. The 3 point hitch at the back allows farmers to quickly attach plows, seeders, cultivators, and other equipment. Meanwhile, the power take-off (PTO) transfers engine power to run machines like threshers, water pumps, or rotavators, increasing the tractor’s versatility.

Operator Comfort & Control

Tractors come with easy to use steering and strong brakes, ensuring safe and smooth operation on any terrain. Ergonomic design features, such as a comfortable seat, simple controls, and good visibility, help farmers work efficiently and comfortably.

Types of Tractors

Tractors come in different types, each designed to handle specific farming tasks and terrains. Understanding the main types helps farmers choose the right tractor for their field and crops.

Type of Tractor Short Summary
Utility Tractor Small to medium sized, used for general farm tasks like plowing, hauling, and transport.
Row Crop Tractor Designed for crops planted in rows; offers high clearance and precision for cultivation.
Orchard/Vineyard Tractor Compact design to work easily between trees or vines without damaging crops.
Industrial Tractor Built for heavy-duty tasks outside farms, like construction, hauling, or landscaping.
Garden Tractor Small tractors for lawns, gardens, and light farming; often used by hobby farmers.
4WD / High-Horsepower Tractor Strong tractors with four-wheel drive for tough terrains and heavy-duty farming operations.
Track / Crawler Tractor Equipped with tracks instead of wheels for soft, muddy, or uneven soil conditions.
Specialty Tractor Designed for specific purposes like sugarcane, tea plantations, or swampy fields.

GST Benefits on Tractors in 2025

Earlier, GST on tractors and agricultural machinery was placed in the 12% to 18% slab, which often increased the overall buying cost for farmers. To support farmers and promote mechanization, the Government of India has slashed GST on tractors and farm equipment to a flat 5%, effective from September 22, 2025. This move has made tractors more affordable, especially for small and medium farmers.

Frequently Asked Questions About New Tractors

What Is The Price Range Of Tractors In India in 2026?

The Price range of tractors in India starts from Rs. 2.44 to Rs. 72.00 Lakh*. Tractor prices vary based on HP, brand, features, transmission, and farming applications.

Which is the most affordable tractor in India?

The most affordable brand new tractor in India is the Swaraj Code, an 11 HP mini-tractor specifically designed for small and marginal farms. It is priced between ₹2.44 Lakh and ₹2.49 Lakh (ex-showroom)

Which are the most popular tractors in India?

The most popular Tractors in India are Swaraj 855 FE, New Holland 3630 TX Special Edition, Swaraj 744 FE, Mahindra Arjun Novo 605 DI i 4WD, John Deere 5050 D, Powertrac Digitrac PP 46i etc.

How Many Tractors are Listed at Tractor junction?

800+ Tractors are Listed at Tractor Junction.

Which tractors are most fuel efficient in India?

The best mileage tractors in India are Swaraj 855 FE, John Deere 5105, Mahindra 475 DI XP Plus and many more.

What is the on-road price of tractors in India?

The on-road tractor price includes ex-showroom price, insurance, RTO charges, and other dealer costs.

Do tractor prices vary by state or city?

Yes, tractor prices may vary based on state taxes, dealer location, transportation, and local offers.

Which tractor offers the best value under ₹7 lakh?

Popular tractors under ₹7 lakh include Mahindra 575 DI, Swaraj 744 FE, and Sonalika DI 35.

Which tractor is best for small farmers in India?

Tractors between 20 HP and 40 HP are considered suitable for small farms and orchard farming.

Which tractor is best for 5 acre farming?

Tractors between 35 HP and 50 HP are suitable for 5 acre farming and regular agricultural work.

Which tractor has the lowest maintenance cost?

Mahindra, Swaraj, and Eicher tractors are popular for low maintenance and easy spare availability.

Which tractors have the highest resale value in India?

Mahindra, John Deere, and Swaraj tractors generally offer strong resale value in the Indian market.

Can I buy a tractor on EMI?

Yes, farmers can buy tractors on EMI through banks, NBFCs, and dealer finance options.

Are government subsidies available on new tractors?

Yes, eligible farmers can get tractor subsidies under various central and state government schemes.

How much diesel does a tractor consume per hour?

Most tractors consume around 3 to 7 litres of diesel per hour depending on usage and HP.

Which tractor is best for rotavator work?

Tractors with strong PTO power and hydraulic lifting capacity are best for rotavator work.

Which tractor is best for haulage purposes?

High torque tractors with strong braking and lifting capacity are preferred for haulage work.

Which tractor is best for sugarcane farming?

Tractors above 45 HP are generally suitable for sugarcane farming and heavy field operations.
